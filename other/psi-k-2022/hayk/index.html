Novel Halide solid state electrolytes


Psi-k 2022

Hayk Zakaryan, Olgert Dallakyan, Misha Aghamalyan, Areg Hunanyan, Yevgeni Mamasakhlisov

Computational Materials Science Lab, Center of Semiconductor Devices and Nanotechnologies, Yerevan State University, Armenia

All solid-state batteries, with solid-state electrolytes, open a floodgate of prospective characteristic properties for future energy storage systems. Although, known solid-state electrolytes (sulfide, oxides, borates, and so on) do not satisfy all performance requirements, such as high ion conductivity or compatibility with anode materials, for wide implementation in various applications. One of perspective solid state electrolytes are halides, due to their high ionic conductivity. Among high ionic conductors are Li3InCl6 with monoclinic (C2/m space group), Li3InBr6 (C2/m) and Li3LaI6 (C2) structure. Stability of these structures were investigated in different compositions like Li-In-Cl, Li-Y-I, Li-La-I. We investigated stability of these structures in the Li-La-H systems, where H=F,Cl,Br,I. Novel stable Li3LaCl6 and Li3LaF6 materials, with monoclinic structure and C2/m Space Group were predicted. Estimation of ionic conductivity shows that Li3LaCl6 can have higher values than known Li3ScI6 and Li3YCl6.
