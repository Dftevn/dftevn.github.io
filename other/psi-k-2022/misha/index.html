Novel 2D TMCs for selective O2, NO, NO2, and nucleobase sensors


Psi-k 2022

Misha Aghamalyan (1), Evgeni Mamasakhlisov (1, 2), Ekaterina Sukhanova (3), Alexander Kvashnin (4), Zakhar Popov (3), Hayk Zakaryan (1)

(1) Yerevan State University, Armenia, (2) Russian–Armenian University, Armenia, (3) Institute of Biochemical Physics, Russian Academy of Sciences, N.M. Emanuel RAS, Russia, (4) Skolkovo Institute of Science and Technology, Russia

While there have already been substantial studies of the several two-dimensional crystals formed from natural crystalline materials with layered structures (graphene, TMDC, and so on), the field's attention is shifting to two-dimensional artificial materials. Theoretical studies are an excellent way to study novel two-dimensional materials with uses in electronics, catalysis, and sensing. Because of the large surface area compared to the bulk size and composition diversity of two-dimensional transition metal chalcogenide nanostructures, they have a wide range of uses in chemical detectors. In the presented study the adsorption of several gas and nucleobase molecules on novel 2D binary structures of metal (Mo, W) chalcogenides (S, Se) was carried out. As a result, review of adsorption energies, charge transfer, conductivity change, selectivity and other properties are presented and compared for novel and established 2D TMCs. New materials are proposed for selective O2, NO, NO2 and nucleobase sensing.
