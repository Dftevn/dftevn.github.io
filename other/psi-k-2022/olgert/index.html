Same structure type, different compositions: computational search of SSE among Li-{In, Ga, La}-{F, Cl, I}


Psi-k 2022

Olgert Dallakyan, Hayk Zakaryan

Computational Materials Science Lab, Center of Semiconductor Devices and Nanotechnologies, Yerevan State University, Armenia

All solid-state batteries with solid state electrolytes open a floodgate of prospective characteristic properties for future energy storage systems. However, known solid state electrolytes (sulfide, oxides, borates, and so on) do not satisfy all performance requirements, such as high ion conductivity or compatibility with anode materials, for wide implementation in various applications. Computational methods can drastically accelerate novel material search, having that such ion transfer parameters are essentially structure dependent. Iodide-type compound Li3LaI6 with stable octahedral Li occupation is found to be a better ionic conductor than any sulfide-type known. Xu et al. found strong relation between Li migration enthalpies in face-centered-cubic anion frameworks and charge to bond length ratio and also lithium coordination number. In current work we study other materials with different compositions but the same iodide-type structure to study the influence of the composition on the relevant properties such as stability and ionic conductivity of the materials.
